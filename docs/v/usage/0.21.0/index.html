zh-TW

選單列多了會跑的神獸，用量越高跑越快

你的選單列現在可以養一隻會動的神獸了。只要打開「召喚神獸」功能，用量百分比旁邊就會跟著一隻白色剪影——Claude 是一隻鳳凰，Codex 是一隻飛龍。牠們的動畫速度會直接反映你的用量：閒置時停住，字數燒得越兇牠就跑得越快。這完全由你本機的用量驅動，不會連網，預設是關閉的。就像在車子的儀表板上放一隻搖頭娃娃，你油門踩得越深，牠頭就搖得越快。

Original changelog
**Summon Spirits — an animated companion in your menu bar**: a new "Summon Spirits" menu item toggles a small white silhouette that runs next to your usage percentages — a phoenix for Claude, a dragon for Codex. It animates faster the harder you burn tokens (idle -> paused, heavy -> sprinting), driven entirely by your local burn rate. Off by default; the on/off state is remembered. No network, like everything else.

總計資料可以直接匯出成 CSV 表格

想把用量資料拿去別的地方用，現在變得非常簡單。你可以直接在終端機輸入 usage export 指令來匯出用量總計，或是從 HTML 報告裡的分享對話框點擊下載，把專案和模型的用量分布匯出成 CSV（一種常見的試算表格式）檔。以前分享按鈕只能複製檔案路徑，現在直接給你整齊的表格。就像以前只能拿到一張列著檔案在哪的紙條，現在直接遞給你一本印好的帳本。

Original changelog
**`usage export` command**: dump your usage totals to CSV straight from the terminal.
**CSV download in the HTML report**: the report's share dialog can now export the project/model breakdown as a CSV file (replacing the old copy-file-path action).

en

Summon animated spirits to your menu bar that sprint when you burn tokens

Your menu bar can now host an animated companion. Simply toggle on "Summon Spirits", and a small white silhouette will run right next to your usage percentages — a phoenix for Claude, and a dragon for Codex. Their animation speed directly mirrors your usage: resting when idle, and sprinting faster the harder you burn through tokens. This is driven entirely by your local usage and makes no network calls, and it's off by default. It's like putting a bobblehead on your car dashboard; the harder you hit the gas pedal, the faster it nods.

Original changelog
**Summon Spirits — an animated companion in your menu bar**: a new "Summon Spirits" menu item toggles a small white silhouette that runs next to your usage percentages — a phoenix for Claude, a dragon for Codex. It animates faster the harder you burn tokens (idle -> paused, heavy -> sprinting), driven entirely by your local burn rate. Off by default; the on/off state is remembered. No network, like everything else.

Export usage totals straight to CSV

If you want to take your usage data elsewhere, it's now incredibly simple. You can simply type the usage export command in your terminal to export your usage totals, or click download from the share dialog inside the HTML report to export your project and model breakdowns as a CSV (a common spreadsheet format) file. Previously, the share button only copied a file path, but now it hands you a perfectly formatted table. It's like going from being handed a sticky note telling you where the filing cabinet is, to being handed a cleanly printed ledger.

Original changelog
**`usage export` command**: dump your usage totals to CSV straight from the terminal.
**CSV download in the HTML report**: the report's share dialog can now export the project/model breakdown as a CSV file (replacing the old copy-file-path action).
